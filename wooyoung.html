Wooyoung

2PM Main Dancer, Lead Vocalist, Fake Maknae, Jang Ang-Ang, Jang Maltese

By: Arianne Mae Apolinario

Jang Wooyoung, not Jung Wooyoung from ATEEZ or Jang Wonyoung from IVE. He is Jang Wooyoung, a 2PM's main dancer and lead vocalist, that is born on April 30, 1989 in Busan. He loved dancing, but he kept this passion hidden from his father (I feel him), who strongly opposed his dream of becoming a dancer because he believed the career lacked stability. In 2007, without telling his family, he applied to first open audition at JYP Entertainment and personally uploaded videos of himself dancing to Mgoon, an early Korean platform similar to YouTube. Competing against thousands of hopefuls, he ultimately ranked first, while future BEAST or HIGHLIGHT member, Yoon Doo-joon, who finished in second place. After relocating to Seoul to start his training with JYP Entertainment, he enrolled at the Seoul Institute of the Arts, majoring in dance. In 2009, he went on to pursue an undergraduate degree in broadcasting at Howon University, which was also attended by his bandmates Lee Jun-ho and Hwang Chan-sung.

In 2008, he gained public attention through Hot Blood Men of Mnet, a survival-style program that followed trainees competing for a place in the group One Day, which ultimately resulted in the formation of 2AM and 2PM. After debuting with 2PM in 2008, where he is the main dancer and lead vocalist. His solo fandom color is blue and in ZOO-PM, he is a chick, that's why everytime only he is not present when the five members gathered, the chick sticker was there in members' IG story. Through his journey with 2PM, he group achieved mainstream success with their second single album 2:00PM Time For Change, leading to a long and influential career marked by multiple Korean and Japanese studio albums, with Jang contributing as a songwriter and composer, including Make It from MUST album, the most recent Korean album of 2PM. Beyond group activities, he has remained active through fan concerts, such as the 2022 joint fan-concert 115430 with Jun. K, and ongoing performances since 2023 as part of the unofficial sub-unit ENWJ alongside Jun. K and Nichkhun.

He launched his solo career in 2012 with the EP 23, Male, Single, which achieved strong chart success and marked the beginning of a steady expansion of his individual artistry through music releases, collaborations, and live performances in both Korea and Japan. Over the years, he released multiple Korean and Japanese EPs and singles, many of which ranked highly on major charts, while also embarking on extensive concert tours that included milestone performances such as his first solo concerts in Korea and shows at prestigious venues like the Nippon Budokan. Alongside music releases, he participated in brand campaigns, charity projects, variety show collaborations, and special digital releases. Following his military service, he continued to evolve as a solo artist with new Japanese and Korean projects, fan meetings, and tours, culminating in recent releases and concerts in 2023 to 2025 that celebrated both his creative growth and major career anniversaries, while expanding his reach to new regions such as Taiwan.

He has built a strong presence in television and variety programming, beginning with his role as an Inkigayo MC from 2009 to 2010 and as an assistant MC on Win Win from KBS. He later gained widespread popularity through variety shows such as We Got Married, web programs like Which Is Better?, and long-running panel and judging roles on music, dance, and competition series including I Can See Your Voice, Joseon Top Singer, Street Man Fighter, Fantasy Boys, and King of Karaoke: VS. From 2022 to 2024, he was a main cast member on Beat Coin, earning a Rookie of the Year nomination, and continued his variety career in 2025 through its successor Screwballs, along with hosting multiple YouTube web shows and co-hosting a dating program, highlighting his versatility as an entertainer, judge, and host.

He made his acting debut in 2011 through the KBS teen drama Dream High, where he portrayed Jason, a Korean-American character, preparing for the role by studying English with his bandmates. That iconic line of Wooyoung as Jason, "Is it my turn already" was so remarkable, especially for HOTTESTS. If we don't know Wooyoung so well, we may think that he is really that fluent in English, given that the Old Boys are known for being fluent in English while the Young Boys, which the category he belongs to, are just cute when speaking English, especially Junho way back then given that he's currently studying English in his post-military era. Anyways, his performance and on-screen romance with IU drew significant attention, earning them a nomination for Best Couple at the 2011 KBS Drama Awards. He later made brief acting appearances, including a cameo with Suzy in Human Casino and a role in the idol-filled short film The Miracle in 2013. In 2024, he shared that although he passed auditions for additional acting projects, he chose to prioritize his music career over pursuing further acting roles.

At first, I wasn't really familiar with him when he appeared on Running Man, especially during Episode 162 when he teamed up with Chansung and Ji Suk Jin, while I only clearly remembered Chansung at the time, to the point that when they had a comeback last 2021, which is the MUST album, I was still figuring out who is Jun. K and Wooyoung, well anyways. I just assumed that since two members of 2PM were guests, they would naturally be very competitive. However, after rewatching his guest appearances, I realized that his approach really depends on his mood that he can be highly competitive or simply play for fun like in the second mission (blob jump mission), he chose to enjoy himself by flipping backward midair, while in other missions he showed his competitive side. He comes across as a carefree member of 2PM who decides whether to go all-in or just enjoy the moment, which was also evident in Episode 256 when they promoted their album No. 5. Beyond variety shows, in 2PM content like 2PM Show, Wild Beat, and Wild Six, he often becomes the reason everyone bursts into laughter due to his unpredictable, out-of-the-box humor. I also find him incredibly cute, so much so that I'd want to pinch his cheeks in person and I call him a "cutie baby." Despite being the oldest in "Young Boys," he acts like the youngest, that's why he is called the "fake maknae" alongside Jun. K who wants to be called "baby" instead of "daddy". When he dances, especially during performances like the sangmo twirl in "My House," he completely amazes me and has even become my bias wrecker. His dancing and freestyle moves are incredibly captivating, living up to his reputation as "Asia’s dancing machine" and the "Korean Justin Timberlake," although his comedic side still shines through, like his hilarious "Single Ladies" performance on Dream Team, which makes his later appearances, such as in Running Man Episode 548, even more entertaining since I already knew he could pull off those moments effortlessly.
